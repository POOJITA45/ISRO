Success Story Of ISRO


The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has transformed from humble beginnings in the 1960s to a global space powerhouse, launching its first satellite, Aryabhata, in 1975. In 2013, ISRO's Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) made India the first nation to reach Mars on its maiden attempt, at a fraction of the cost of similar missions. The 2019 Chandrayaan-2 orbiter discovered water molecules on the Moon, paving the way for future explorations despite the lander setback. Chandrayaan-3's successful soft landing near the lunar south pole in 2023 made India the fourth country to land on the Moon and the first to do so in that region.

Aditya-L1, launched in 2023, became India's first solar observatory at the L1 point, providing continuous data on solar activity to study space weather and coronal mass ejections. The 2024 Gaganyaan test flights advanced India's human spaceflight program, with unmanned missions validating crew module recovery and life support systems, targeting crewed flight by 2025.
