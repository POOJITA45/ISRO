ISRO

Indian Space Research Organisation

OVERVIEW:

The main space center of ISRO is the Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC), located in Sriharikota, Andhra Pradesh, which serves as India's spaceport for launching satellites and rockets.SDSC is the primary launch base for ISRO missions, equipped with multiple launch pads for rockets such as PSLV, GSLV, and specialized missions including lunar and Mars probes.The center houses two main operational launch pads for orbital missions and a separate pad for sounding rockets Facilities at SDSC SHAR include vehicle assembly, static test and evaluation for rocket motors, propellant processing plants, telemetry and command networks, and a viewing gallery for public launch events. SDSC has infrastructure for both solid and liquid propellant rocket preparation, testing and launch, and has expanded to support heavier boosters for missions like Gaganyaan. India's space program is supported by an integrated network of specialized ISRO centers, with SDSC SHAR as the launch hub and VSSC and SAC as major contributors to research, development, and applications.

Timeline of ISRO

  • 1962:

    INCOSPAR (Indian National Committee for Space Research) was founded under Dr. Vikram Sarabhai, laying the foundation for space research and forming early technical committees.


  • 1963:

    The first rocket launch took place at Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station (TERLS), Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala‚ÄîIndia‚Äôs first space center dedicated to sounding rockets and atmospheric studies.


  • 1965:

    Space Science and Technology Centre (SSTC), which later became part of the Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre (VSSC), was set up in Thiruvananthapuram to focus on rocket and satellite technology development.


  • 1967-1968:

    Satellite Communication Earth Stations were established in Ahmedabad, Gujarat, supporting experimental satellite and telecommunication activities.


  • 1969:

    ISRO was officially formed, centralizing space programs and preparing for indigenous technology development. ISRO's headquarters were set up in Bengaluru.


  • 1970s-1980s:

    The launch vehicle program led to the creation of more centers: Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre (VSSC), Thiruvananthapuram for launch vehicle development. Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC), Sriharikota, Andhra Pradesh as India's main spaceport for rocket launches starting in 1971.


  • 2019:

    ISRO subsequently established new centers such as the Liquid Propulsion Systems Centre (LPSC), U R Rao Satellite Centre, the Human Space Flight Centre (HSFC), and regional support facilities as the space program grew.
